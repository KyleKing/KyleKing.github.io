Summary

At the University of Maryland, with a team of 4 fellow senior bioengineering students, we created an immersive experience device for pediatric patients. Typically, when an MRI is necessary, pediatric patients are given anesthesia; however, there is a growing body of literature suggesting that this practice can lead to short term and possibly long term neurological deficits, along with additional cost, risks for respiratory issues, and other immediate concerns. Our approach was to create a device that follows the user from the hallway/waiting room to inside the MRI and all the way back out.

The Experience

Since no magnetic materials can enter the MRI room, we designed a three step approach. The first is an iPod touch that establishes the story and allows for the child to make selections for the rest of their experience. Once ready to enter the MRI room, the iPod is removed and replaced with a the static transparency is visible. Without the iPod, the child can be moved to the MRI bed, but will still be entertained. Our static transparency is similar to the View-Master experience and could include a Where's Waldo game or a calming ocean scene to extend the story established by the iPod experience. THe third step is a projector system. Many MRI's have a long throw projector that is just outside the room and projects through a hole in the wall to a screen behind the MRI. By removing the screen, the projector can be extended directly onto a special projector screen on the goggles. Once finished, the process can be reversed as the child is brought back out to the hallway.

Transition-Friendly Design

To allow a the technician to swap between the three modes seamlessly, we designed a paddle system that could allow quick modifications, but would still secure each successive item. First, the goggles were assembled with the phone slid in place and the static transparency snapped onto the front of the goggles. Once on the child's face, the phone could be slid out and the static image would be visible. Once on the MRI bed, the projector screens could be slid in, then the transparency slid off minimizing the down time in between transitions.
