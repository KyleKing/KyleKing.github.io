Design-Based Reboot of the Site

May 10th

This week, I started working on the fourth iteration of the website. So far it started from a very rough wireframe on paper and built in ... Then the website progressed into a hard-coded design with several iterations and a couple weekends of coding, which could probably be summer up into two iterations.

The next one is probably the most-focused on design. As a new member of startup shell, I've been exposed to flat design and have caught up on the past ten years of popular design. Pretty much every app and most new websites feature the shadowless, minimalistic, and trendy color schemes iconic of flat design.

Simple things like the don't color to the type of font weren't considered in the last version of the site, so this one will feature big improvements in those accords.

On a less software side, my bike is nearly functioning and ready for some rides this week. Just put a BikeUMD sticker to take across the country with me!

Building the Backend

May 18th

Building a website has two sides. One is the front-end, which is a loosely defined term that refers to the look of the website. The colors, the navigation and the layout all are important to this. Three main languages make up the front end experience. HTML is used to create the content (I use Jade, which generates an HTML file, but is more efficient to type and edit). CSS is used to make the visual layout work from the color palette, font choices, to the actions when you click or hover. JQuery and JS are the animation and more technical parts of the site.

The past few months have focused on building this part of the website as I was learning it all as I worked. From today I am beginning to learn the back-end, which will help collect, sort, and display all the data, such as GPS Coordinates, speed and more. I'm learning how to use MySQL and PHP to improve the site's maintenance.

 

Putting the "Micro" in Microelectronics

May 20th

Working on the hardware for this summer is so exciting. I really couldn't thank Microduino enough for providing one of the coolest sensor kits that somehow is smaller than my mobile tool. The size and power of this device blows my mind. I'm excited to see such innovation being so open to interested people.

The kit Microduino sent has a core+ as the main processing device. This is controlled by a computer connected to the FT202R module. The core then controls the bottom weather station, OLED display, 10DoF module, GPS receiver, and an SD card for storage.

All of this is tiny. Like really tiny. Smaller than an Arduino Uno (which is equivalent to only two of the modules).

Finishing Sophomore Year

May 21st

It's going to be a whirlwind trip back home. I finished up all my finals within twelve hours on Friday, the second day of finals week, but stayed until the very last moments to spend time with friends and push my independent study as far as I could take it this semester. Heading home Wednesday meant that I only have a week and a half home until send off in just 11 days! I'm going to need to make the most of this time to get the website, database, and hardware all in harmony and functioning. I am bringing my laptop and will likely still be doing maintenance and improvements, but I would like to be able to focus on the ride as much as possible.

I can't believe my sophomore year has come to a close. I feel like I've been in college for years. So much has happened in just two years. I've met so many new friends, yet disconnected and lost many. Others I have built stronger bonds with and will spend the next two years struggling through the hardest classes UMD has to throw at us. I couldn't be more happy with the Gemstone team that I was placed on or the QUEST team who just demolished the other competition this semester. After a full semester of recruiting, the medical device team I started last year finally has the right mix of talent and dedicated members. I can't wait to see what problems we overcome as a team.

80 More Miles of Testing

May 24th

On my first 80 mile training ride! Going to bike the entire NCR trail and back again. Left my house at 6:50, hoping to get to the trailhead by 7:30. Thankfully we made it traffic-free and now have plenty of time!

The ride started off nice and slow staying together as a group. We but the Mason-Dixon Line around noon and all took selfies to commemorate the distance. This was my first time ever biking into PA on the NCR. I had always given up much earlier in the route.

After the PA line, we took a break to eat lunch in New Freedom. The trail was packed with people outside walking their dogs, on their bikes, or just out for a hike. We all couldn't have had nicer weather or a more pleasant day.

From here we headed to York at full speed. Splitting up as a group, Santi and I continued on at a brisk pace keeping almost 20 MPH as we rushed to finish the NCR before we both had to go at 4 pm. The first fifteen miles went without incident, but with five miles to go we both began burning out.
